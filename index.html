Flavor Network

This interactive network shows which ingredients go well together. The basic ideas behind this are “Flavor pairing(Food Pairing Theory)” and “Umami pairing”. “Flavor pairing” suggests that different ingredients are more likely to pair well in a recipe when they share share many flavor compounds. The main umami compounds are glutamate, inosinate and guanylate, and “Umami pairing” suggests that umami is sensed strongly when they are combined.


For further information on “food pairing theory” and the data set used for constructing the network, please see the below paper:

"Flavor network and the principles of food pairing" Yong-Yeol Ahn, Sebastian E. Ahnert, James P. Bagrow and Albert-László Barabási
